Nelson Liu

I have a proposal to make for this group. I would like to be president of IHS scioly.

If I cannot bring in 23 GOLDS at the next years Regionals, please feel free to FIRE me. #1FORINTERLAKE

Naomi Gradinaru

Science Olympiad is a big priority for me, and I'm willing to go the extra mile to ensure that we succeed next year. As an officer, I will support the creation of a build squad comprised of about 5 students that focus their efforts towards build events, and I will work towards finding adult mentors for study events. I will also make the commons more available as a space to work on builds and as a space to have team bonding activities.

Andrew Xue

I believe in making things easy for our members. I will make sure competition dates, schedules, and requirements are posted early. I will abolish last-minute switching of events without consent. I will always be at meetings to answer questions.

Patrick Liu

I wouldn't fix what ain't broken, so I would keep event sets, tryouts, and other A team-related business with minimal changes. However, I would try a completely different strategy for the elements that didn't work so well this year—particularly the management of newcomers and builds. Here's to experimentation!

Lukas Corey

If elected, I plan to make this club a community. In order to minimize previous issues with changes in event sets and people leaving, I'll treat people (especially freshmen!) with a friendly attitude, and I'll work to develop better organization of events to be able to compensate for people leaving without complete switching of events. I will work to get forms out earlier and bug people so that we don't have to rush them in at the last minute and I look forward to working with such an awesome group of people.

Erich Liang

Do you want an experienced, dedicated, and fabulous officer? Do you want someone who can help double your performance, triple your fun, and half your studying time? Then vote Erich Liang for Science Olympiad officer!

Daniel Hou

Making Nationals is critical next year. I can deliver that with my proven track record of high commitment and my expertise in planning, organizing, coordinating, and leading our teams. The ideas I ran on last year were incredibly successful this year—the number of medaling A Team members exploded from just 2 to all 15—and I want to continue that trend next year. Do you?
